Chess.

A Timeless Game

Setup.

The Gateway to Mastery

To get started, you'll need a chess board and a friend (optional).

In chess, players alternate turns, with the player controlling the white pieces always starting first. This continues until a player wins or a draw occurs. There are many ways that chess games can end, but most commonly, somebody wins by checkmate or both players agree to a draw.

We'll explain checkmate in more detail later. For now, let's explore how each piece moves.

The Pawn.

Small, Yet Mighty

The pawn is the most basic piece with simple movement rules:

Each pawn can only move forward, one square at a time, except on its very first move where it has the option to move two squares forward.

Pawns may capture enemy pieces directly diagonally in front of them, taking their place.

When a pawn reaches the opposite end of the board, it is promoted to any other piece (except a king), usually a queen.
